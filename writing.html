Cycle

The days have been passing in the blink of an eye. Friends become strangers, and strangers become friends. I become a stranger to myself. My 'self' grows old and stagnant, then blooms into a new self, as if it was shedding its' skin occasionally.

You evolve, then become worse, then become better... and on it loops. You will love the person you were a few years ago, then hate it, then love yourself as of now, then hate yourself as of now, and shed it again. You get new interests, then discard them, then rediscover them. You get new companions, then discard them, then rediscover them. You get a new self, then discard it, then rediscover it.

Skin shedding. Name shedding. Am I in the process of shedding my skin yet again? Probably. There is no constant 'self', regardless. I am stuck in this agonizing bot-like cycle, repeating the errands of assimilation and constant self-evaluation... justifying this existence with doing pointless chores each day, regular media consumption in the form of animated or book catharsis, talking to myself in circles, of the superficial belief of some sort of accomplishment being done when nothing has been done to contribute towards anything meaningful. Words never amount to the same extent that actions do, so this too, was pointless yet again.

Oh well!

Love

What is love? (Baby don't hurt me)

Jokes aside, I wonder what it truly is. I don't really get a lot of feelings in general, they feel alien to me. I don't know if this is due to Alexithymia or just inexperience. How do relationships work? Do people feel something when they spend time together? Can it be properly explained? I struggle to even know if I feel love for my family, even though I feel grateful and appreciative of them. Human emotions are very difficult, huh... I envy people who can easily feel strong emotions.

Beyond just exchanging information, laughing about jokes and discussing interests, I don't see much of a point to friendships personally, so I have a harder time figuring out what romantic relationships even entail aside from physical contact with one another.

I do doubt it's something like "aromanticism" since I do have fantasies and derive enjoyment from romantic media, but I have zero desire to act out on them. What a conundrum...

Solitude

I guess the reason why I find that animals are well off and content in living is because of the lack of human commitments they have to put up with, human existentialism, relationships, friendships, nurturing, everything like that.
It's innately human to desire a social circle to be a part of and to have ones presence be accepted, maybe essential and helpful even to those around. This can apply to one-on-one relationships too; we want to feel some sort of justification for existing, whether it may be through talking, creating (arts, etc), or helping.

But personally, I think you'll only grow to be dependent on other people for that happiness you seek. It's not wrong to want to be happy, especially by trying to be by connecting with other people, but these connections are impermanent and can fade away, doubts and speculation can build up. I find all of these aspects of friendships to be very stressful to maintain and communicate, so if anything, I treat human interactions as a secondary need rather than a primary one.

It's coming to terms with the fact that I'll probably always be in solitude and not ever go to the cinema with a hypothetical friend, or ever have an intimate discussion with someone, nor ever ask my classmates for help with homework. I'll likely never experience these sort of normal human interactions and I've grown to be OK with that. I want to build my sense of self-worth through my own efforts and aspirations, and not through someone else's. Doing so, I find it easier to be alone and to never be lonely.

People who don't really think about anything too much sort of intimidate me. I'm not sure why.

Is it because they can get close to people without regretting it?

Is it because they can get through life without conflict?

Is it because they're fine with having people know of their existence?

Whatever it is, people like that intimidate me, because they surely wouldn't welcome my overthinking nature. They only would want people who'd give them a laugh with an amusing crowd.

I think people who don't really think too much are one of the smarter ones, emotionally wise. They never have to worry too much about anything.

It's much smarter to not be the way I am, to not think. That's why I am an idiot and the others are more socially and mentally well-adjusted.

Puritanism

To risk sounding pretentious; I'm not particularly into the whole moral judgement thing. I never understood that sort of thing. I think it's okay to not agree with people on some opinions but I don't particularly enjoy those who think their judgement is absolute and if you disagree with them, you're a villain that needs to be stopped.

Of course, I prefer to avoid people that makes me uncomfortable, but I won't go out of my way to change their opinion about a subject. The reality is that you can't decide what people should or shouldn't say, it's not for you or anyone else to control. You should be more concerned with yourself than what other people think.

This isn't really about modern puritans although it is applicable. Throughout history, there have always been people trying to control people under the guise of ethics when in reality they just want to feel in control and righteous for enforcing their beliefs.

It's fine to stand up for what you think is right and bring awareness, but you can't ever force anyone to think or believe something. That's for them to decide.

That kind of person

I don't want to go down in history. I don't want to be someone.

Yet, I have some lingering feelings of wanting to leave something meaningful behind.

I want to be remembered as a "nobody", someone who was just a passing phase in people's lives.

I want to be someone people don't think of when they think of someone they enjoy hanging out with. Someone that fades into the background. Irrelevancy.

I want to make things, but not have these creations be remembered for me but for the creations itself.

Maybe it's a lonely kind of desire, but I find the idea of someone still thinking of me 10 years later a way more terrifying prospect.

I want to talk to people, but not with the intent of getting closer, but to kill time.
